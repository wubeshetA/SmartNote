Introduction Microorganisms

    • Organisms that are too small to be seen with the naked eye.
    • Exist in various environments, including soil, water, and the human body.
    • They have diverse shapes and characteristics.
    • Can be beneficial or harmful to other organisms.
    • Essential for various biogeochemical cycles.
    • Used in industries for the production of medicines, food, and more.
    • Some have resistance to certain antibiotics and drugs.
    • Can adapt to extreme environments, like hot springs and the deep sea.

    Types:

    • Bacteria:
      • Unicellular microorganisms with a simple cellular structure.
      • Can be found in diverse environments.
      • Some are beneficial, aiding in digestion and nutrient absorption.
      • Others can cause diseases like tuberculosis and pneumonia.
      • Antibiotic-resistant bacteria are a growing concern in healthcare.
    • Viruses:
      • Consist of genetic material (DNA or RNA) surrounded by a protein shell.
      • They invade cells of living organisms to reproduce.
      • Known for causing diseases like flu, AIDS, and COVID-19.
      • Vaccines are often developed to boost immunity against viruses.
    • Fungi:
      • Include yeast, molds, and mushrooms.
      • Some fungi are edible while others can be toxic.
      • Fungi play a role in decomposition, returning nutrients to the soil.
      • They can also cause infections, especially in immunocompromised individuals.
      • Used in brewing, baking, and medicine production.
